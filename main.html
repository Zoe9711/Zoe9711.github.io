FOR NOW THERE IS ONLY
The Greeks had no single term to express what we mean by the word “life.” They used two terms that, although traceable to a common etymological root, are semantically and morphologically distinct: βῐ́ος, which indicated the form or way of living proper to an individual or a group. And ζωή, which expressed the simple fact of living common to all living beings.
